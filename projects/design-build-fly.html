Design Build Fly (DBF)

Landing Gear Lead

Overview

Led landing gear design for the team’s RC plane. Evaluated multiple concepts (retracts, sliding pins) against constraints and figures of merit. Final design: fully-removable, one-piece landing gear with canopy pin locks; pins are not load-bearing.

Options considered

  • Option 1 (Retracts) — Rejected. Could not fully retract into fuselage or wings; would not fit in shipping crate with vertical stabilizer attached (would require retraction along two axes).
  • Option 2 (Sliding pins) — Rejected. Quick attachment but added considerable stress on mount (would require fully aluminum build), created shear points on pins, and would require custom vertical pins.

Final design

  • Fully-removable, one-piece landing gear
  • Housed within fuselage and under wing spar (cut-out during fuselage manufacturing)
  • Pushes up against wing spar during landing
  • Landing gear (both struts connected) sandwiched between two layers of the mount (white block in diagram)
  • Canopy pin locks: pins only hold the gear in the aircraft during flight — pins are not load-bearing; strength comes from the mount and spar interface
